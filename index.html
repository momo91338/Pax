What Does This Page Do?

This tool converts an image into a pixelated design divided into sections, ideal for:

  • Hand embroidery patterns
  • Paper or pixel art
  • Mosaic designs
  • Any project that needs a simplified grid-based version of an image

How It Works

  1. Upload an Image: Choose an image from your device to preview it.
  2. Set Your Preferences:
    • Number of Sections: Split the image into 1, 4, 9, 16, or 25 parts.
    • Number of Colors: Limit the number of used colors (for easier crafting).
    • Grid Size: Controls the resolution of each section.
    • Second Color: The tool generates a gradient from white to this color.
  3. Convert the Image: The image is split, pixelated, and colors are reduced. A color chart is generated showing:
    • Color number
    • HEX code
    • Mix with white
    • Color usage count

Why Use This Tool?

  • Easy to recreate designs by hand
  • Clear color table
  • Divides large designs into manageable sections

From Mosaic to Pixel: When Ancient Art Intersects with Modern Technology

Since ancient times, humans have sought to capture their world in visual form. Long before photography, mosaic art flourished in Roman and Byzantine civilizations. Artists arranged tiny colored stones or glass pieces into stunning visuals—each fragment acting like a primitive pixel.

In today’s digital age, photography is built upon the same foundation: millions of pixels aligned to form detailed images. Although the tools have changed, the essence remains. It is still about assembling small units of color to tell a bigger story.

A perfect example of this artistic bridge is seen through tools like Pax, which transforms modern images into pixelated forms reminiscent of mosaic patterns. With a simple upload, even the Mona Lisa transforms into a digital mosaic, connecting the past with the present in a single frame.
